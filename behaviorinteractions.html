Behavioral Interactions

I am studying how animals change their behavior in the presences of each other

The results from my 2015 study on the interactions between spiders, grasshoppers, and rolly-polies. Strangely, rolly-polies cause the spider to grasshopper effect on soil nitrogen mineralization (Nmin) to disappear, as indicated by the arrow.

Behavioral Interactions

I became interested in the behavioral interactions between grasshoppers, spiders, and rolly-polies after I finished analyzing the data for my Master's Thesis. The data showed that the strong effect of spiders Pisaurina mira onto grasshoppers Melanoplus femurrubrum disappeared when the isopod Oniscus asellus was present. Normally these spiders scare the grasshopper but don't normally get to eat a lot of them. The data suggested that the isopods were causing the spiders to eat more grasshoppers meaning their strong effect on soil nitrogen mineralization caused by grasshoppers stress-eattng the plants disappeared.

I wondered why the isopods were making the spiders better at catching grasshoppers. To answer this question I have been conducting behavioral studies examining the behavior of spiders and grasshoppers in cages to see where and how they spend their time. I hypothesized that the isopods were scaring the grasshoppers and making it more likely that they ran into spiders.

Looking for grasshoppers and spiders in the leaf litter of a behavioral observation cage. Despite the cage being small these animals are very sneaky and hard to spot!

The results produced another surprise: it was the spiders not the grasshoppers that were moving becuase of the isopods. The grasshoppers like to hang out high up on the plants during the day, but the spiders prefer lower in the canopy to avoid the hot sun. However, when the isopods were present the spiders were more likely to move upwards and more likely to encounter and eat the grasshoppers. I think this is because these spiders hunt by sitting and waiting in one place for their prey. They don't eat isopods, but might be disturbed by them and decide to just climb a little higher to avoid wasting energy reacting to the isopods. I am working on follow-up studies now to see how these interactions impact the ecosystem.

Curriculum vitae

My research focuses on the role of animals, plants, and microorganisms play in the movement or cycling of elements through ecosystems. I use a combination of mathematical theory and experiments to refine our understanding of how nutrients move through our environment. My research aims to improve our response to changes in either element cycling or biotic communities (e.g. invasive species, species loss) by documenting how interactions between animals, plants, and microorganisms change each other's relatoinship with elemental cycles.

Current Research Projects

Herbivore - Decomposer Feedbacks

I am working in Connecticut old-fields to understand how animals such as grasshoppers, rolly-pollies, and earthworms jointly impact ecosystems.

Fear of Predation in Decomposers

Many animals experience fear in the presence of a predator and we are looking to see how this impacts detritivores (animals that eat dead things).

Theory in Coupled Ecosystems

I am using models of carbon and nitrogen cycling to test how animals influence whole-ecosystem nutrient cycling.

Microbes in Ecosystem Models

I am working with soil scientists, ecologists, and ecosystem modelers to improve how we model microbes and soil animals.

Decomposer Natural History

I am surveying the abundance and studying the lives of some common decomposers in Connecticut.

Behavioral Interactions

I am studying how animals change their behavior in one another's presence.
